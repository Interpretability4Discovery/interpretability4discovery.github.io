Submission guidelines

Submissions must present original, unpublished work that has not appeared at NeurIPS or another archival machine-learning venue. Submissions are managed through OpenReview and remain private during review.

Paper submissions4 pages

Main text only. References and appendices do not count toward the limit, but the main text must be self-contained.

Formatting

  • Write the submission in English and upload it as a single PDF.
  • Use the NeurIPS 2026 workshop LaTeX template.
  • References and appendices are excluded from the four-page main-text limit.
  • The main text must be self-contained; reviewers are not required to read appendices.

Submissions and presentation

  • Research and position papers may be methodological, empirical, theoretical, or perspective-driven.
  • Accepted papers will be presented as posters, with a subset selected for oral or spotlight talks.

Review and publication policies

Double-blind review

Remove author names, affiliations, and acknowledgments. Cite prior work in the third person.

Non-archival

Work under review elsewhere is welcome, and accepted work may be published at another venue afterward.

Responsible-use statement

Every submission must include a short statement covering potential societal impacts and suggested mitigations. A missing statement is grounds for desk rejection.

Original work

Work already published at NeurIPS or another archival machine-learning venue should not be submitted.

Contact

Questions may be sent to interp4discovery@gmail.com.
